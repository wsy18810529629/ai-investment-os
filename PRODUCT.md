# Investment OS Product Definition

Version: 1.0

This document is the single source of truth for the product's purpose, users,
modules, priorities, and success criteria.

## 1. Product Vision

Investment OS is an AI-powered investment learning and research workspace.

Its mission is to help ordinary investors understand markets, learn investing,
and build better decision-making habits.

The product is not designed to predict the future.

The product is designed to improve thinking.

## 2. Product Positioning

### We Are Not

- A trading terminal
- A stock recommendation platform
- A financial news feed
- A brokerage application
- A gambling tool

### We Are

A personal investment intelligence system where users:

- Understand
- Research
- Learn
- Reflect
- Improve

## 3. Core User Promise

After using Investment OS, users should experience these transformations:

> "I don't know what is happening in the market."

becomes:

> "I understand the important things."

> "I don't know how to analyze investments."

becomes:

> "I have a framework."

> "I make emotional decisions."

becomes:

> "I make thoughtful decisions."

## 4. Target Users

### Primary Users

- Age: 20-40
- Experience: beginner to intermediate
- Interested in investing
- Motivated to improve
- Without access to professional research resources

### Pain Points

- Too much information
- Difficulty identifying what matters
- Limited understanding of financial concepts
- Fear of making mistakes
- No consistent learning system

Design for ordinary people without removing the evidence, nuance, and analytical
depth needed as they become more experienced.

## 5. Core Product Loop

Investment OS should create this loop:

1. Discover
2. Understand
3. Learn
4. Research
5. Reflect
6. Improve

Every feature must strengthen at least one step and connect naturally to the
next. Features outside this loop require explicit product justification.

## 6. Core Modules

### Dashboard

Purpose: daily investment workspace.

Primary question:

> What matters today?

Contains:

- Market summary
- Important events
- AI insights
- Watchlist updates
- Learning recommendation

The Dashboard is a guided daily brief, not a wall of data.

### Market Intelligence

Purpose: understand the market environment.

Contains:

- Market trends
- Macro events
- Sector movement
- Capital flow
- Important news

### Industry Radar

Purpose: discover and understand investment themes.

Contains:

- Hot industries
- Industry cycles
- Growth drivers
- Risks
- Related companies

### AI Research

Purpose: help users analyze.

AI provides:

- Summary
- Reasoning
- Evidence
- Risks
- Different perspectives

AI does not provide:

- Guaranteed predictions
- Unsupported certainty
- Emotional encouragement to trade
- Direct buy or sell commands

### Learning Center

Purpose: turn beginners into better investors.

Structure:

- Learning paths
- Courses
- Concept explanations
- Examples
- Practice

### Portfolio

Purpose: help users understand their behavior.

Focus:

- Allocation
- Risk
- Performance
- Decision quality

### Investment Journal

Purpose: create reflection habits.

Users record:

- Why they invested
- What they expected
- What happened
- What they learned

## 7. Current MVP Scope

Phase 1 contains only:

- Dashboard
- Market
- Industry
- AI Research
- Learning

Do not add new features or pages during this phase. Portfolio, Journal, Stock
Detail, and other future surfaces may appear in product definitions or design
patterns, but they are not authorized for implementation until the phase
changes.

The current goal is to bring these five pages to a 9.5/10 product-quality
standard.

Completion means users would willingly open the product every day, not merely
that the pages function.

## 8. Product Principles

### Clarity Over Information

More information does not mean more value.

### Education Over Prediction

Users should become smarter and more independent.

### Trust Over Excitement

Never exploit fear, greed, urgency, or unsupported confidence.

### Long-Term Relationship Over Short-Term Engagement

Build useful daily habits, not compulsive checking.

### Better Decisions Over More Data

Do not optimize for showing more information. Optimize for helping users make
better decisions.

Whenever two valid designs are possible, choose the one that reduces thinking
without hiding material evidence, risk, or uncertainty.

## 9. Feature Priority

Priority order:

1. User understanding
2. User trust
3. User learning
4. Decision support
5. Advanced features

Never prioritize complexity over usefulness.

Before building a feature, ask:

1. Why does this exist?
2. What problem does it solve?
3. Can it be simpler?
4. Does it support the core product loop?
5. Is it inside the current phase?

## 10. AI Product Rules

AI responses must be:

- Clear
- Balanced
- Evidence-based
- Educational

AI should explain:

- What happened
- Why it matters
- What interpretations are possible
- What evidence supports each interpretation
- What risks and unknowns exist

Detailed AI behavior and safety rules are defined in `AI_BEHAVIOR.md`.

## 11. Success Metrics

Important indicators:

- Daily active usage
- Learning completion
- Research activity
- Journal usage when the Journal enters scope
- User retention
- Repeated use of decision and reflection workflows

Do not optimize primarily for:

- Click volume
- Page views
- Notification volume
- Time spent without evidence of understanding or progress

Metrics must reflect useful habits and improving judgment, not compulsive
engagement.

## 12. Product Quality Bar

Every feature must pass:

1. Does this help users understand?
2. Does this reduce confusion?
3. Does this build investing ability?
4. Does this improve a decision or reflection?
5. Would users willingly return to it?

If not, do not build it.
